Page 01

Purpose:
Introduce the problem — foundation models are stateless, so without memory an agent re-solves the same task from scratch every time and never improves.

Audience note:
Assumes only that the reader knows an "AI agent" tries to do tasks. No prior memory/ML vocabulary needed.

Panel Count:
6

Image Prompt:

Create a six-panel educational manga page.

Style:
Soft-color educational manga.
Light watercolor / gentle anime palette — warm cream paper, soft ink linework, muted pastel washes.
One restrained accent: recall-green (#00e0a4), used only for memory, recall, and "it worked" moments.
Clean confident linework, generous negative space, friendly but precise.
Calm, contemplative, explanatory tone — diagrams integrated naturally into the scene.
Not gritty. Not neon. Not chibi. Not glossy 3D. Not photorealistic.

Characters in this page:
- ENGY: a small, rounded librarian-robot with a soft matte-cream chassis, one big friendly camera-lens eye that glows a faint recall-green, short stubby arms, and an open NOTEBOOK embedded in its chest — that notebook is its memory; its pages go blank and grey when Engy forgets.
- SENSEI: a calm middle-aged human archivist with round glasses, a soft cardigan over a high-collar shirt, neat greying hair, holding a slim wooden pointer.

Setting:
A warm, softly lit study with a small practice maze/puzzle table where Engy attempts a simple task.

Panel 1 (wide establishing):
Engy stands before a simple wooden puzzle-maze on a table, Sensei beside it with the wooden pointer. Calm morning light. Dialogue space for Sensei to set the scene.

Panel 2 (medium):
Engy solves the little maze — its eye glows soft recall-green, the chest-notebook briefly fills with green ink. A tiny success spark. "It worked!"

Panel 3 (close on the chest-notebook):
Overnight / a beat later: the notebook pages flutter and go BLANK and grey. Everything Engy just learned drains away. No green left.

Panel 4 (medium, repeat):
A new identical maze on the table. Engy stares at it as if for the first time, eye dim, no recognition. Body language: starting over.

Panel 5 (close on Engy, slightly comic frustration):
Engy re-solving the same maze laboriously, sweat-drop, the very same fumbles as before — clearly no carry-over.

Panel 6 (two-shot, Sensei explains):
Sensei taps the blank notebook with the pointer, calm. The thesis of the page. Dialogue space for Sensei.

Camera directions:
Open wide and warm; tighten to the notebook on panel 3 for the "forgetting" beat; close on Engy's repeated effort; end on Sensei's quiet diagnosis.

Lettering:
Leave room for English dialogue and captions.
- Panel 1 — Sensei: "Watch. Engy will solve this little maze."
- Panel 2 — Engy: "Done! That was easy."
- Panel 3 — (caption) "But a model with no memory is *stateless* — afterwards, the notebook empties."
- Panel 4 — Engy: "...Have I seen this before?"
- Panel 5 — Engy: "Why does it feel so hard *again*?"
- Panel 6 — Sensei: "With no memory, every task starts from zero. You can't keep what you can't remember."

Output:
A single finished manga page.

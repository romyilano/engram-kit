Page 02

Purpose:
The common fix — give the agent a memory module so it can store and reuse past experience. Set up the catch: who decides the shape of that memory?

Audience note:
Builds on page 1 (statelessness). Introduces "memory module" as simply "a place to keep notes."

Panel Count:
6

Image Prompt:

Create a six-panel educational manga page.

Style:
Soft-color educational manga.
Light watercolor / gentle anime palette — warm cream paper, soft ink linework, muted pastel washes.
One restrained accent: recall-green (#00e0a4), used only for memory, recall, and "it worked" moments.
Clean confident linework, generous negative space, friendly but precise.
Calm, contemplative, explanatory tone — diagrams integrated naturally into the scene.
Not gritty. Not neon. Not chibi. Not glossy 3D. Not photorealistic.

Characters in this page:
- ENGY: a small, rounded librarian-robot with a soft matte-cream chassis, one big friendly camera-lens eye that glows a faint recall-green, short stubby arms, and an open NOTEBOOK embedded in its chest — that notebook is its memory; pages fill with soft green ink and flutter when Engy remembers.
- SENSEI: a calm middle-aged human archivist with round glasses, a soft cardigan over a high-collar shirt, neat greying hair, holding a slim wooden pointer.

Setting:
The same warm study; now a small wooden card-catalog / note-box sits beside Engy, representing the memory module.

Panel 1 (medium):
Sensei sets a neat little card-catalog box next to Engy and gestures: this is a memory module. Engy looks intrigued. Dialogue space for Sensei.

Panel 2 (close on the chest-notebook):
Engy solves a task; this time a note is written and KEPT — the notebook page fills with soft green ink and stays. A small filing-card drops into the catalog box.

Panel 3 (medium, a new but related task):
A new puzzle appears. Engy flips back through its notebook, finds a relevant green note, eye brightening with recognition.

Panel 4 (action):
Engy reuses the remembered strategy and solves the new task faster — a confident recall-green glow. "I remember this kind!"

Panel 5 (two-shot):
Sensei nods, then raises the pointer with a gentle "but..." — turning the catalog box so we see its rigid, pre-printed dividers and fixed slots.

Panel 6 (close, the hook):
Tight on the fixed dividers of the catalog box; a question mark hovers. Who decided the SHAPE of this memory — the slots, what gets filed where, what gets thrown away?

Camera directions:
Warm and steady; emphasize the green "kept note" on panels 2–4; end by drawing attention to the box's rigid structure as the cliffhanger.

Lettering:
Leave room for English dialogue and captions.
- Panel 1 — Sensei: "So we give Engy a *memory module* — a place to keep and reuse what it learns."
- Panel 2 — (caption) "Now the note survives. Stored experience can be reused."
- Panel 3 — Engy: "Wait — I've done something like this before..."
- Panel 4 — Engy: "Faster this time! Memory helps."
- Panel 5 — Sensei: "It does. But look closely at the *box* itself..."
- Panel 6 — (caption) "Who designed these slots — what to keep, where to file it, what to forget?"

Output:
A single finished manga page.

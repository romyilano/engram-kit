Page 04

Purpose:
The core idea — ALMA's Meta Agent meta-learns the memory *design* through an open-ended loop: Sample → Reflect → Ideate & Plan → Implement → Verify → Evaluate → Archive. This is the centerpiece of the whole story.

Audience note:
Builds on page 3 (fixed designs fail). Introduces the Meta Agent as "an agent that designs memories instead of doing the task." Treat the loop as a cycle the reader can follow step by step.

Panel Count:
7

Image Prompt:

Create a seven-panel educational manga page laid out as a clear CYCLE (panels arranged so the eye loops back to the start; subtle recall-green arrows connect each panel to the next, and the last arrow curves back to the first).

Style:
Soft-color educational manga.
Light watercolor / gentle anime palette — warm cream paper, soft ink linework, muted pastel washes.
One restrained accent: recall-green (#00e0a4), used only for memory, recall, and "it worked" moments.
Clean confident linework, generous negative space, friendly but precise.
Calm, contemplative, explanatory tone — diagrams integrated naturally into the scene.
Not gritty. Not neon. Not chibi. Not glossy 3D. Not photorealistic.

Characters in this page:
- THE META AGENT: a tall, elegant architect-figure made of soft luminous recall-green light, calm featureless features, holding a glowing blueprint in one hand and a quill that writes lines of code in the other; a faint blueprint-grid shimmers around it.
- ENGY: a small, rounded librarian-robot with a soft matte-cream chassis, one big recall-green camera-lens eye, stubby arms, an open NOTEBOOK in its chest (its memory).
- THE ARCHIVE: a tall wall of glowing drawer-cards, each card pairing a small code-schematic with a little success-rate gauge.

Setting:
A luminous workshop. The Archive wall on one side; Engy on a small task-stage on the other; the Meta Agent moving between them.

Panel 1 — SAMPLE:
The Meta Agent slides one glowing drawer-card out of the Archive wall — a past memory design with its success-rate gauge. Recall-green arrow points onward. Label space: "1 · SAMPLE."

Panel 2 — REFLECT:
The Meta Agent studies the sampled card's code and evaluation log, the blueprint unfolding in the air; it reads the gauge ("Success: 0.3"). Thoughtful. Label: "2 · REFLECT."

Panel 3 — IDEATE & PLAN:
A soft burst of ideas around the Meta Agent's head; it sketches a modification plan on the floating blueprint. Label: "3 · IDEATE & PLAN."

Panel 4 — IMPLEMENT:
The quill writes actual lines of code; the new memory design takes shape as a small code-schematic. Label: "4 · IMPLEMENT (in code)."

Panel 5 — VERIFY:
A quick correctness check — a tiny green check confirms the code runs / is valid before it's used. Label: "5 · VERIFY."

Panel 6 — EVALUATE:
The new design is installed into Engy's chest-notebook; Engy runs a real task; a new evaluation log + gauge appears ("Success: 0.4"). Label: "6 · EVALUATE on tasks."

Panel 7 — ARCHIVE (loops back):
The Meta Agent files the new design + its log back into the Archive wall as a fresh glowing card; the curving recall-green arrow returns to Panel 1. Label: "7 · STORE → repeat."

Camera directions:
Keep each panel a clean single beat with its step-label; the connecting recall-green arrows are essential so the reader feels the loop; the final arrow visibly curves back to panel 1.

Lettering:
Leave room for English dialogue and captions and the step labels (1–7).
- Caption (top): "ALMA doesn't do the task. It *designs the memory* — over and over."
- Panel 2 — Meta Agent: "This old design only scored 0.3. Why?"
- Panel 3 — Meta Agent: "What if we change how it retrieves and updates?"
- Panel 6 — (caption) "Try it on a real task: 0.3 → 0.4."
- Panel 7 — (caption) "Keep the result. Sample again. This open-ended loop never stops improving."

Output:
A single finished manga page.

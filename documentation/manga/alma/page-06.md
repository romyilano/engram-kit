Page 06

Purpose:
The archive — every explored design is stored with its evaluation log; the Meta Agent samples from this archive to seed new ideas, so good designs propagate and the search compounds over time (open-ended exploration).

Audience note:
Builds on pages 4–5. Reinforce that the archive is *memory about memory designs* — it's how the search gets smarter, not random.

Panel Count:
6

Image Prompt:

Create a six-panel educational manga page.

Style:
Soft-color educational manga.
Light watercolor / gentle anime palette — warm cream paper, soft ink linework, muted pastel washes.
One restrained accent: recall-green (#00e0a4), used only for memory, recall, and "it worked" moments.
Clean confident linework, generous negative space, friendly but precise.
Calm, contemplative, explanatory tone — diagrams integrated naturally into the scene.
Not gritty. Not neon. Not chibi. Not glossy 3D. Not photorealistic.

Characters in this page:
- THE ARCHIVE: a tall wall of glowing drawer-cards; each card pairs a small code-schematic (a memory design) with a little success-rate gauge (its evaluation log); better designs glow brighter recall-green.
- THE META AGENT: a tall, elegant architect-figure made of soft luminous recall-green light, holding a glowing blueprint and a code-writing quill.
- ENGY: a small, rounded librarian-robot with a soft matte-cream chassis, one big recall-green camera-lens eye, an open NOTEBOOK in its chest (its memory) — appears small in a corner running a task.

Setting:
A grand archive hall — the wall of drawer-cards dominates; soft light, library calm.

Panel 1 (wide establishing):
The full Archive wall: rows of glowing drawer-cards, each with a tiny schematic + success-rate gauge. Some glow brightly (good), some dimly (weak). Dialogue/caption space.

Panel 2 (close on a few cards):
Detail of three cards side by side: a faint one (0.2), a medium one (0.4), a bright one (0.7) — the reader sees designs are scored by how well they did.

Panel 3 (action):
The Meta Agent reaches in and SAMPLES — pulls a promising bright card forward; it unfolds into a blueprint to reflect on. (Echoes page 4 step 1, now from the archive's point of view.)

Panel 4 (medium):
From that sampled design, the Meta Agent sketches a NEW improved variant — a child design branching off, glowing a touch brighter.

Panel 5 (medium):
The new design + its fresh evaluation log are filed back as a new card; the wall has grown by one, and the brightest region of the wall spreads. Good ideas propagate.

Panel 6 (two-shot, the point):
The Meta Agent gestures across the steadily brightening wall while tiny Engy succeeds at a task in the corner. The archive is what makes the search compound. Dialogue space.

Camera directions:
Establish the wall wide, then go close to read the gauges, then show the sample→improve→refile motion; end on the wall visibly brighter than panel 1.

Lettering:
Leave room for English dialogue and captions.
- Panel 1 — (caption) "Every design ever tried is kept here — with its score."
- Panel 2 — (caption) "0.2... 0.4... 0.7 — the archive remembers what worked."
- Panel 3 — Meta Agent: "Start from a design that already did well."
- Panel 4 — Meta Agent: "...then make it better."
- Panel 5 — (caption) "File the result back. Good designs seed even better ones."
- Panel 6 — Meta Agent: "This is open-ended search — it compounds. It never has to stop."

Output:
A single finished manga page.

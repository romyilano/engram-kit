Page 07

Purpose:
Results — ALMA's learned memory designs were evaluated across four sequential decision-making domains and consistently beat state-of-the-art human-crafted baselines, while also being more cost-efficient.

Audience note:
Builds on pages 4–6. The four domains are testbeds for "learning from experience." Keep the names but explain them as little worlds.

Panel Count:
6

Image Prompt:

Create a six-panel educational manga page.

Style:
Soft-color educational manga.
Light watercolor / gentle anime palette — warm cream paper, soft ink linework, muted pastel washes.
One restrained accent: recall-green (#00e0a4), used only for memory, recall, and "it worked" moments.
Clean confident linework, generous negative space, friendly but precise.
Calm, contemplative, explanatory tone — diagrams integrated naturally into the scene.
Not gritty. Not neon. Not chibi. Not glossy 3D. Not photorealistic.

Characters in this page:
- ENGY: a small, rounded librarian-robot with a soft matte-cream chassis, one big recall-green camera-lens eye, stubby arms, and an open NOTEBOOK in its chest (its memory), which now holds a design tailored to each world.
- THE META AGENT: a tall, elegant architect-figure made of soft luminous recall-green light, holding a glowing blueprint and a code quill (present quietly as the designer).
- SENSEI: a calm middle-aged human archivist with round glasses, a soft cardigan, neat greying hair, holding a slim wooden pointer.

Setting:
Four small "worlds" shown as cozy dioramas, then a simple comparison chart.

Panel 1 (four small insets in one panel, or a 2x2 cluster):
Four little worlds, each labeled: ALFWorld (a tidy household-chores room), TextWorld (a world made of words/parchment), Baba Is AI (a tile/rule-pushing puzzle), MiniHack (a small dungeon). Engy stands ready in each.

Panel 2 (medium):
In each world, the Meta Agent hands Engy a *different* tailored memory design — the chest-notebook reconfigures per world. Soft recall-green fits.

Panel 3 (action montage):
Engy succeeding across the worlds — learning from experience, reusing what it stored, eye glowing recall-green. Confident.

Panel 4 (clean comparison chart, integrated as a board Sensei points to):
A simple bar chart: "Human-crafted memory" bars vs. "ALMA-learned memory" bars — the ALMA bars are taller (better) on every world. Recall-green for the ALMA bars.

Panel 5 (second small chart):
A tiny cost gauge: ALMA-learned designs also use FEWER resources than most human-crafted baselines — efficient as well as effective. Recall-green check.

Panel 6 (two-shot):
Sensei taps the chart with the pointer; Engy beams. The result lands. Dialogue space.

Camera directions:
Keep the four worlds distinct and readable; make the comparison chart the clear payoff panel; end warm on Sensei + Engy.

Lettering:
Leave room for English dialogue and captions.
- Panel 1 — (caption) "Four very different worlds: ALFWorld · TextWorld · Baba Is AI · MiniHack."
- Panel 2 — Meta Agent: "Each world, its own memory design."
- Panel 3 — Engy: "I'm actually learning from experience now!"
- Panel 4 — Sensei: "Across every benchmark, the *learned* designs beat the hand-crafted ones."
- Panel 5 — (caption) "And they cost less to run."
- Panel 6 — Sensei: "Better memory — discovered, not hand-drawn."

Output:
A single finished manga page.

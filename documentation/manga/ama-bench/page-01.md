Page 01

Purpose:
Setup — LLM agents now run long, multi-step tasks, and memory is what keeps them coherent across a long journey. Introduce Engy on a long road.

Audience note:
Assumes only that the reader knows an "AI agent" does multi-step tasks. "Long-horizon" = a task with many steps over a long stretch.

Panel Count:
6

Image Prompt:

Create a six-panel educational manga page.

Style:
Soft-color educational manga.
Light watercolor / gentle anime palette — warm cream paper, soft ink linework, muted pastel washes.
One restrained accent: recall-green (#00e0a4), used only for memory, recall, and "it worked" moments.
Clean confident linework, generous negative space, friendly but precise.
Calm, contemplative, explanatory tone — diagrams integrated naturally into the scene.
Not gritty. Not neon. Not chibi. Not glossy 3D. Not photorealistic.

Characters in this page:
- ENGY: a small, rounded librarian-robot with a soft matte-cream chassis, one big friendly camera-lens eye that glows a faint recall-green, short stubby arms, an open NOTEBOOK in its chest (its memory), and a small satchel for collecting scrolls.
- SENSEI: a calm middle-aged human archivist with round glasses, a soft cardigan over a high-collar shirt, neat greying hair, holding a slim wooden pointer.

Setting:
The mouth of a very long winding road that disappears over the horizon — the metaphor for a long-horizon task. Soft dawn light.

Panel 1 (wide establishing):
Engy stands at the start of the long winding road, satchel ready, Sensei beside it pointing the wooden pointer toward the far horizon. Dialogue space for Sensei.

Panel 2 (medium):
A small signpost near Engy lists steps of a long task (e.g. "find → open → read → fix → check → report"). Many steps, one long journey. Engy reads it.

Panel 3 (close on Engy's notebook):
Engy takes the first few steps and jots the first notes; the chest-notebook begins to fill with soft green ink. Memory starting.

Panel 4 (medium, time passing):
Further down the road; the start is now tiny behind Engy. The question forms: will it still remember what happened way back at the beginning?

Panel 5 (close, the stakes):
Engy pauses, looking back at the faint distant start, eye flickering — a hint of the difficulty of holding a whole long journey in memory.

Panel 6 (two-shot, thesis):
Sensei rests the pointer on Engy's shoulder. The page's point: on a long road, memory is everything. Dialogue space.

Camera directions:
Lead with the long road to establish scale; use the shrinking start behind Engy to convey "long-horizon"; end on a steady two-shot.

Lettering:
Leave room for English dialogue and captions.
- Panel 1 — Sensei: "Today's agents don't do one step. They walk long roads — many steps, far from where they began."
- Panel 2 — Engy: "Find, open, read, fix, check, report... that's a lot to hold onto."
- Panel 3 — (caption) "Each step becomes a note in memory."
- Panel 4 — Engy: "The start feels so far away now..."
- Panel 5 — Engy: "Will I still remember how this began?"
- Panel 6 — Sensei: "On a long road, *memory* is what keeps you coherent. So — how do we test it?"

Output:
A single finished manga page.

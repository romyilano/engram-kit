Page 03

Purpose:
AMA-Bench's construction — a real-world subset (expert QA across six domains) plus a synthetic subset (programmatic, rule-based QA) that scales to any horizon length.

Audience note:
Builds on page 2. "Subset" = one half of the test set. Real-world = curated by experts; synthetic = generated by rules so it can be made arbitrarily long.

Panel Count:
6

Image Prompt:

Create a six-panel educational manga page.

Style:
Soft-color educational manga.
Light watercolor / gentle anime palette — warm cream paper, soft ink linework, muted pastel washes.
One restrained accent: recall-green (#00e0a4), used only for memory, recall, and "it worked" moments.
Clean confident linework, generous negative space, friendly but precise.
Calm, contemplative, explanatory tone — diagrams integrated naturally into the scene.
Not gritty. Not neon. Not chibi. Not glossy 3D. Not photorealistic.

Characters in this page:
- THE EXAMINER: a poised proctor figure holding a fan of question-cards, each stamped with a category seal; soft-toned robes; a small balance-scale emblem at the collar; a quiet scoreboard slate.
- ENGY: a small, rounded librarian-robot with a soft matte-cream chassis, one big recall-green camera-lens eye, stubby arms, an open NOTEBOOK in its chest, and a small satchel.
- SENSEI: a calm middle-aged human archivist with round glasses, a soft cardigan, neat greying hair, holding a slim wooden pointer.

Setting:
A calm examination hall. The Examiner stands at a desk that splits visually into two halves — a "real-world" half and a "synthetic" half.

Panel 1 (medium establishing):
The Examiner reveals TWO neat stacks of question-cards on the split desk, labeled "REAL-WORLD" and "SYNTHETIC." Engy and Sensei watch. Dialogue space.

Panel 2 (left half — real-world, six domains):
Six small domain icons fan out from the real-world stack: Web, open-world QA, Text2SQL (a database), Software Engineering (code), Gaming (a controller), Embodied AI (a little robot arm). A small "expert-checked" stamp.

Panel 3 (close, real-world detail):
A real-world card up close: drawn from an actual agent trajectory, with an expert-written question and answer. Caption: curated and sanity-checked by people.

Panel 4 (right half — synthetic):
The synthetic stack pours out of a small "rule engine" — cards generated automatically in a programmatic environment, while keeping the same agent-environment interaction pattern.

Panel 5 (medium, scalability):
The synthetic road stretches and stretches — a dial labeled "horizon length" turns up, and the road grows arbitrarily long. Synthetic = scalable to any length.

Panel 6 (two-shot, the point):
The Examiner holds one card from each stack; Sensei nods. Two complementary halves: realistic + scalable. Dialogue space.

Camera directions:
Keep the real-world/synthetic split legible throughout; make the six domain icons clear in panel 2; use the lengthening road in panel 5 to show scalability.

Lettering:
Leave room for English dialogue and captions.
- Panel 1 — Examiner: "AMA-Bench has two halves."
- Panel 2 — (caption) "Real-world: six domains — Web, open-world QA, Text2SQL, software engineering, gaming, embodied AI."
- Panel 3 — Sensei: "Real trajectories, with questions written and checked by experts."
- Panel 4 — (caption) "Synthetic: rule-generated tasks in programmatic environments."
- Panel 5 — Examiner: "And synthetic tasks scale to *any* horizon length."
- Panel 6 — Sensei: "Realistic *and* scalable — that's the design."

Output:
A single finished manga page.
